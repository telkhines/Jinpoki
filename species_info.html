A Short Guide to Jinpoki

A simple summary guide to Jinpoki, and their traits.

A comprehensive guide is currently in development. As Jinpoki are constantly mutating, it's hard to capture a full list of traits.



The Mother Jinpoki

The Mother, also known as the first Jinpoki, is a 500+ foot space entity. She travels great swathes of space to settle in underground cave systems of different planets. Once settled, she begins to devour the thoughts and emotions of the planet's inhabitants for food. Once sated, she begins her travels again.

This alien entity has terrible eyesight and is rather clumsy. When traveling, she tends to bump rocks and asteroids, unintentionally crash landing on various different celestial bodies.

These accidents can harm and tear her apart, though any pieces removed from her main form reproduce asexually much like planarian worms. They may also grow into smaller parasitic aliens; which are referred to as colony Jinpoki.

The mother has been long dead for thousands of centuries, passing because of old age. Her carcass is preserved in Jinpoki's home planet of Xaoc, deep in an underground cave.



Jinpoki Sizes

A simple Jinpoki size chart through the centuries of evolution.

The Mother Jinpoki was around 500+ feet long, with her colony being approximately 40 to 70 feet long.

When the Mother died, the colony started to deform creating a multitute of variations and mutations. Over time, they gradually decreased in size to approximately 20 to 30 feet in length.

As centuries passed, the modern Jinpoki developed into their current state. They are roughly 2 to 10 feet in height, having a more humanoid shape and build, with human-level intelligence.



Jinpoki Food and Growth

All Jinpoki appear to be unisex in appearance, with no visible reproductive organs. In terms of reproduction, they mate in similar ways to fish and snails and are Monoecious; having two types of reproductive organs.

As for food and growing up--

Much like their ancestor, the Mother, Jinpoki feed off of thoughts and emotions; a single touch or bump would be enough for a Jinpoki to extract some of their "prey's" emotional essence. When first hatched from the egg, Jinpoki parents tend to visit crowded areas to gather enough emotions to feed their budding offspring until it becomes fully independent.

Jinpoki don't brainwash individuals they feed off of, they do however, cause deja vu, presque vu, jamais vu, and many other instances of sense-memory loss.

Modern Jinpoki have learned to control their hunger with the help of general various foods. Emotions and thoughts are treated as healthy snacks! This is in stark contrast to ancestral Jinpoki, which could only subsist on the thoughts and emotions of other beings.
